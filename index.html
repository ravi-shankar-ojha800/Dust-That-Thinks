05

Theories of Consciousness

01

Global Workspace Theory

Bernard Baars / Stanislas Dehaene

Mind contains many unconscious processors. Consciousness occurs when information enters a global broadcast workspace — like a theater spotlight.

C = Broadcast(Selected Information)
02

Integrated Information Theory

Giulio Tononi

Consciousness corresponds to integrated information (Φ) in a system. Higher Φ = more integrated causal unity = more consciousness.

Φ > 0
03

Predictive Processing

Karl Friston / Andy Clark

The brain does not passively perceive reality — it predicts it. Perception equals prediction plus error correction.

Perception = Prediction + Error
04

Orch-OR

Penrose / Hameroff

Consciousness may involve quantum collapse in microtubules. Controversial but raises profound questions about physics and mind.

Quantum → Classical Collapse
05

Emergence Theory

Various

Consciousness emerges from sufficiently complex information processing. The whole becomes greater than the sum of neural parts.

Complexity → Phenomenology
